💼 Fake Internship Scams

High Risk

🚨 Common Tactics

  • •Posting on job portals with fake company names and logos
  • •Promising certificates from "IIT", "IIM" or "ISO certified" companies
  • •Collecting registration/training fees before onboarding
  • •Ghosting after payment is made
  • •No real work — just tasks to extract data from the student

✅ How to Verify

  • •Search the company on MCA portal (mca.gov.in)
  • •Check reviews on Glassdoor or AmbitionBox
  • •Apply only through official portals: Internshala, LinkedIn, Naukri
  • •Real companies NEVER ask for payment to join
  • •Use ScamShield to analyze the job posting

📱 WhatsApp Recruiter Scams

Very Common

🚨 Red Flag: "WhatsApp HR"

If a recruiter contacts you ONLY via WhatsApp and has no official email, LinkedIn profile, or company website — it is almost always a scam. Legitimate companies use official corporate email addresses and have verifiable online presences.

Task-Based Scams

Asks you to complete "tasks" like liking YouTube videos or rating products for ₹500-₹1000. After some payments, they ask you to deposit a larger amount to "unlock" bigger earnings — and then disappear.

Data Collection Scams

Uses the "job" as a pretext to collect your Aadhaar, PAN, bank details, or selfie. This data is then used for identity fraud or sold to cybercriminals.

Fake Interview Scams

Sends a fake offer letter over WhatsApp. Before joining, asks for a "refundable" security deposit. After payment, the "company" becomes unreachable.

💬 Telegram Job Scams

Rising Threat

How Telegram Scams Work

  1. You receive an unsolicited message on Telegram offering easy money
  2. Added to a group with fake "testimonials" of people earning thousands
  3. Given a small task, paid a small amount to build trust
  4. Offered a "premium plan" for bigger earnings — requires deposit
  5. Deposit is taken, you are blocked

Warning Signs

  • 🔴Unsolicited job offers from unknown numbers
  • 🔴Groups with 1000+ members, all posting income screenshots
  • 🔴Promises of ₹2000–₹5000/day for simple tasks
  • 🔴Requires UPI/bank transfer before you can receive payment
  • 🔴No physical office, no verifiable company details

💸 Advance Fee Fraud

Classic Scam

⚠️ The Golden Rule

No legitimate employer will ever ask you to pay money to get a job. Any request for registration fees, security deposits, training fees, or equipment charges before or during the hiring process is a scam — period.

📝
Registration Fee
Paid to "register" for a job. Never legitimate.
🔒
Security Deposit
"Refundable" deposit for equipment or access.
📚
Training Fee
Paid for "mandatory" training before joining.
💻
Equipment Cost
Buy a laptop/kit from the "company" to start.

🪪 Identity Theft Scams

Dangerous

Fraudsters use job applications as a pretext to collect sensitive personal documents that are then used for financial fraud, SIM swapping, and identity theft.

Documents They Target

  • ❌Aadhaar Card (front + back)
  • ❌PAN Card
  • ❌Bank account / IFSC details
  • ❌Selfie holding ID documents
  • ❌Video of yourself saying specific phrases

Safe Sharing Guidelines

  • ✅Only share documents after a formal offer letter
  • ✅Always watermark documents with company name and date
  • ✅Verify company CIN on mca.gov.in first
  • ✅Never share OTPs — not even to "HR"
  • ✅Report to cybercrime.gov.in if documents were shared

🛡️ Prevention Tips & Safety Resources

Essential Reading
🔍

Before Applying

  • • Research company on Google
  • • Check LinkedIn page and followers
  • • Verify domain age and SSL
  • • Run job posting through ScamShield
  • • Read Glassdoor reviews
💬

During Interview

  • • Insist on video call interviews
  • • Ask for official email communication
  • • Verify interviewer on LinkedIn
  • • Never pay any fees at this stage
  • • Ask for physical office address
📋

After Getting Offer

  • • Read the offer letter carefully
  • • Verify company registration number
  • • Confirm office visit before joining
  • • Never pay to join — report if asked
  • • Inform family/friends about the offer
